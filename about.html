About me

When I was a kid, I took a course in HTML4 alongside my dad, and I built a static webpage to show off scans of my Pokemon cards to the world. I remember enjoying the way I could tell the screen what to do with specific characters and preset rules. It was the same reason I loved Legos and role-playing games: they presented a system with finite parameters and endless possibilities.

In college, my favorite course was Infographics 484, a core experience that led to my degree in graphic design. I loved the intersection of data and story telling. The fun for me was finding new ways to visually express information that was not visual, like putting together Lego blocks I’d never seen before.

My career as a graphic designer has given me the opportunity to explore different media and test my skills. I’ve learned that what I love is the challenge of telling stories within set parameters, from coding landing pages within a company brand, to writing science fiction universes, to creating infographics about the amount of water in clouds. It’s hard to wrap that passion into a single term, so I hope my portfolio can speak more to what I love doing and doing well.
